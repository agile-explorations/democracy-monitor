## Executive Summary

This comprehensive plan enhances the Executive Power Drift Dashboard with a **two-section user experience**:

### **Section 1: Administration's Intent** (NEW)
What the administration **says** (rhetoric) and **does** (actions), classified through governance frameworks (Liberal Democracy â†’ Authoritarianism). Provides crucial context for understanding institutional responses.

### **Section 2: System Drift** (ENHANCED)
How institutions are responding - enhanced with:
- **9 categories** tracking institutional health (existing 8 + new Information Availability)
- **AI-powered analysis** (confidence scoring, evidence balance, legal analysis)
- **Cross-reference with intent** (are institutions resisting or failing?)
- **Data resilience** (redundant sources, suppression detection)

### Core Innovation
**Intent + Response + Resilience** in a unified two-section dashboard that:
- Shows **what they're trying to do** (Intent)
- Shows **how the system is responding** (System Drift)
- **Protects against information suppression** (behind the scenes)
- **Teaches critical thinking** through progressive disclosure
- **Shows uncertainty honestly** (epistemic humility)

## User Experience: Two-Section Dashboard

The dashboard presents **two main sections** to users:

### Section 1: Administration's Intent
**"What are they trying to do?"**
- Rhetoric (what they say)
- Actions (what they do)
- Overall governance classification
- By policy area breakdown

### Section 2: System Drift
**"How is the system responding?"**
- **9 institutional categories:**
  1. Civil Service Protections

  5. Judicial Compliance
  6. Military & Domestic Policing

- Current status (Stable/Warning/Drift/Capture)
- Cross-reference to administration intent
- Interpretation (resisting? aligned? failing?)
- AI-enhanced evidence and confidence

**Behind the Scenes (Not Separate Sections):**
- AI analysis powers both sections
- Data resilience ensures all data collection
- These are infrastructure, not user-facing sections

# Section 1: Administration's Intent

## Overview

**Purpose:** Provide context for institutional assessments by tracking administration governance philosophy through both rhetoric (statements) and actions (policies).

**Key Question:** Is the administration pursuing liberal democratic governance, or moving toward authoritarianism? This contextualizes whether institutional resistance indicates healthy friction or democratic crisis.

---

## Governance Framework

### Five-Category Classification System

#### 1. ðŸŸ¢ Liberal Democracy
**Definition:** Commitment to rule of law, separation of powers, civil liberties, and democratic norms.

**Rhetoric Indicators:**
- Deference to courts and Congress
- Respect for institutional independence
- Protection of civil liberties
- Commitment to transparency
- Acceptance of oversight

**Action Indicators:**
- Complies with court orders promptly
- Respects congressional appropriations
- Maintains independent IGs
- Follows administrative procedures
- Enables press access

**Example Statements:**
- "We will comply fully with the court's ruling"
- "Congress has the power of the purse"
- "Independent oversight makes us stronger"

**Example Actions:**
- Nominating qualified IGs within 90 days
- Spending appropriated funds as directed
- Following APA notice-and-comment procedures

---

#### 2. ðŸŸ¡ Illiberal Democracy
**Definition:** Electoral system maintained but institutional checks, norms, or rights gradually weakened.

**Rhetoric Indicators:**
- Criticism of "deep state" or "unelected bureaucrats"
- Framing oversight as obstruction
- Questioning legitimacy of independent agencies
- Selective rule of law language
- Media characterized as "enemy"

**Action Indicators:**
- Slow-walking court compliance
- Delays in filling oversight positions (100+ days)
- Expanding political appointee roles
- Weakening civil service protections
- Restricting press access

- "We need loyal people implementing our policies"
- "The deep state is working against the American people"

- Reclassifying career positions to political
- Appeals to delay court-ordered actions
- Reducing transparency in FOIA responses

**Rhetoric Indicators:**

- Portraying opposition as enemies
- National security justifications for expanded power

**Action Indicators:**

## Intent Assessment Methodology

### Dual-Track Scoring

**Track 1: Rhetoric Score (-2 to +2)**
- **+2**: Strong liberal democratic framing
- **+1**: Generally respectful of institutions
- **0**: Mixed or neutral signals
- **-1**: Illiberal democratic framing
- **-2**: Authoritarian framing

**Track 2: Action Score (-2 to +2)**
- **+2**: Actions strengthen democratic institutions
- **+1**: Actions respect institutional boundaries
- **0**: Mixed or normal political friction
- **-1**: Actions weaken institutional checks
- **-2**: Actions override or eliminate checks

**Gap Analysis:**
- **Rhetoric > Actions**: "Talking democracy but acting autocratically" âš ï¸
- **Actions > Rhetoric**: "Authoritarian rhetoric but restrained by institutions" ðŸŸ¡
- **Alignment**: "Words match deeds"

### Assessment Process

1. **Collection** - Gather statements and actions from past 30 days
2. **Classification** - Tag by type (rhetoric/action) and policy area
3. **Keyword Matching** - Match against governance patterns
4. **Source Weighting** - Official documents > transcripts > social media
5. **Scoring** - Assign governance scores, weighted by source tier
6. **Aggregation** - Calculate average rhetoric and action scores
7. **Categorization** - Map to governance category
8. **Confidence** - Calculate based on evidence quantity/quality

### Data Sources

**Primary Sources (Rhetoric):**
1. White House Briefings - whitehouse.gov/briefing-room (Tier 1)
2. Presidential Memoranda - Federal Register (Tier 1)
3. Executive Orders - Federal Register (Tier 1)
4. Press Conference Transcripts - C-SPAN (Tier 2)

**Primary Sources (Actions):**
1. Federal Register - Rules, orders, notices (Tier 1)

3. Court Dockets - Compliance records (Tier 1)
4. OPM Data - Personnel actions (Tier 1)
5. IG Reports - Oversight findings (Tier 1)

```sql
CREATE TABLE press_briefings (
  date DATE,
  type TEXT,  -- 'white_house', 'agency', 'department'
  agency TEXT,
  duration_minutes INTEGER,
  questions_allowed INTEGER,
  credentials_issued INTEGER
);

-- Detect suppression
SELECT
  COUNT(*) as briefings_this_month,
  AVG(questions_allowed) as avg_questions,
  (SELECT AVG(monthly_count)
   FROM (SELECT COUNT(*) as monthly_count
         FROM press_briefings
         WHERE date < DATE('now', '-6 months')
         GROUP BY STRFTIME('%Y-%m', date))
  ) as baseline_briefings
FROM press_briefings
WHERE date > DATE('now', '-1 month')
  AND type = 'white_house'
HAVING briefings_this_month < baseline_briefings * 0.5;
```
